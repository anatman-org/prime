i remember

    there is no question
    i love her deeply ... 

    i keep remembering her body,
    her nakedness,
    the day at Wygal’s,
    and it haunts me ...

    i am enslaved
    to the need for her body

the place downstairs from my flat
in the Sop

called Wygal's
but everyone calls it, "The Tooth"

it is a hard bar for the hard workers of the Sop
and the longer and louder drums bleed

through the floors of my building
  she would later confess

  "i knew you lived upstairs
  i followed you home one day

  pretending i had a delivery"
i laugh

today, though
i wake in the shining sun

hungry as a horse
makes sense i was up all last night

my bed is cold, wet
exhausted,

i must have sweat through
the heat of the day

change the sheets
                          fresh sheets

                          making waves
                          a surface, a shape, familiar

hungry as a horse
i toast some bread and slather it with honey

    some insect is trapped, banging against the window
i let it out

the smell of the Sop -- damp, mushrooms --
pouring in

it is Sixday
no class today

    but the Sop always
    bustles and jostles

if there had been class
i would have gladly lost the credits

to sleep in
                          something is happening, opening

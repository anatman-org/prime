# The Annotated Manual to the Glass Bead Game

The Game has always had one and one objective only,
and that is the Honest Investigation of Reality.

This investigation defies the biases that come with one time and place,
and so the players of the game dig deep into languages forgotten, imagined, and unimagined.
And out of that has emerged, through countless centuries of refinement,
a game of glass beads.

Compared most often to the art of music,
the Game is a composition of glass beads, arranged in a sequence
to express something.

In this, players will often harmonize deeply,
resonating as it were,
with their environment.
That is,
players listen intently,
and play the Glass beads as if to express the otherwise inexpressible,
but to associate it as much with experiences of others.

Equally important to the Players, though,
is the Observers.
These people watch the games and analyze them,
like archivists, critical reviewers, admirers and foes, 
and replaying these games as they analyze and review
the experience of the players
  that inexpressible
becomes a part of the Observer.
Not a memory, but a memory of a memory,
and more importantly
  a memory of a personal reaction -- aversion or admiration -- to the other's memory.

Did the player convey the experience adquately?
What even was that experience?
Knowing the answers to these questions
with any great certainty is,
  of course,
a reflection of the different points of knowledge accumulated
between any two times and places.

So the interesting thing is then
to find the sequence of game operations
that one might play
if one wanted to close the gap
between two places and times.

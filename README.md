# The Annotated Manual to the Glass Bead Game

# Dedication

It is said that, "To learn the Game is easy, to live it, hard.
The search for what is real is no simple matter."

This investigation is the essence of the Game.

In this respect, whether one uses fifty glass beads on a surface,
or sticks or yarrow stalks or coins or cards,
or the empirical method, or a deep faith, or a burning love,
or nothing at all,
it doesn't matter.

All that matters is curiousity.

And it is the sworn duty of the Magister Ludi
to foster the growth of knowledge for as we say in our oaths,

    this is the Sacred Obligation of Castalia
    the Vow, the Promise We have made
    to Expand Knowledge through the Education of others.

This same vow, that Magister Ludi Joseph Knecht has reminded Us of,
has no obligations, no boundaries, no restrictions.
Our job is to Expand Knowledge through the Education of others.
Considerations of wealth and fame bear no meaning to One who truly understands the Game.

What does That look like?

This is the particular line of Knowledge
that the Magister Ludi is committed to Expanding.

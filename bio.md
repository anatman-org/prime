# Story of the name "bean"

Every once in a while, someone is born with the name "Bean"
but that is not my real name.

My real name is xa.Qaree.Bin.ia,
or in CE Xabinia Qareeb.

When i was born,
my mother died.

i learned later how this changed
the dynamic of my family.
Why my older brother would torture me.
Why my father would ignore me.
It was my fault, after all, right?

And as he would torture me, my brother would shorten my name, slur it to
"bean," which in that time and place was a pretty bad slur.
It simultaneously meant something dirty, from the dirt,
and it also meant a woman's clitoris.
In the sexist shame-culture of that time and place,
being of the Earth
and being the center of pleasure for half of humanity
were somehow "dirty" and "scandalous" and "unspeakable" in nature.

As I grew older and as I tried to separate myself from the torture,
to liberate myself from first the violent domination of my brother,
then the silent reproach of my father,
and then the contempt and ignorance of my instructors
and the jealousy of my rank and order peers.

And along every path of liberation, of relief,
the one that offered the most freedom was that of Mathematics and Game Theory.
So I pursued it, with all that I was.

And I one day learned that grand master level players
refer to the glass beads as "beans"
in a machismo, obscene sort of joke.

  by that rank, I was the only girl among my class group -- one of twenty-five --
  They all looked at me, expecting a reaction
  and of course i reacted to the them looking at me.

  And i started to cry.

  And afterward, i was always called "bean" behind my back.
  Never to my face, the cowards.
  Sometimes it is okay to hate what wants to be evil, to cause suffering to others.

So when i woke up, pulling myself off the street,
a light, shaped by the outline of clear, blue sky, clouds,
the eyes of the one everyone knows as Feather
Her dark, dark Eyes pierced me
and Our lives were woven together.
And when She sang "bean" She was talking about me.

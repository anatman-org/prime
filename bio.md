
an old man who sees clearly

    that he is the butt of every joke
    that he is the perfect example of privilege and enlightenment

    and people said "he could do anything if he had only put his mind to it, but he never will"
    or they said, "he's not as smart as he thinks he is"

    and they could never know
    how truly right they were

    not really, not the way
    he knew they were right

    and he was afraid

until he acknowledged
and accepted the fact that
the best thing he can do
is be useless

    no one needs
    his advice
    his expertise
    his experience
    his bullshit

    no one wants
    his spoiled and ugly privilege
    his smugness
    his lying and two-facedness

and he was curious
"what does this mean?"


and wondering and reading
he did the best he could think to do with all that spoiled privilege

to dream ... to ask oneself the last of the questions,
the one that comes at the end of the story

    acknowledging that i am the paradox of both wasted AND infinitesimaly-small talent
    and a deeply flawed person of my own design

and so probing into all the deep Truths he could find,
throwing away one or another -- for all good reasons that he has written down --

came to that final question of existence


and it is frightening, yes, but sitting in this fear
and knowing one's own fear is to truly know oneself

and this is the essence of mind, of that question
of why is it that

sadness is as heavy as
    a pile of bricks

happiness is as light as
    a feather

why is it so hard
to put down sadness from our own minds?

why is it so hard
for us to carry happiness for long in our lives?

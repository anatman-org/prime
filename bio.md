Hypatia said,

  i am the one at the end of the story, the "Afsana"
  and it is the greatest of the stories to tell

and i am the last Magister Ludi before her.

My name is Martin Root.
It is a coincidence that,
as Magister Ludi,
I am granted exclusive access to the root account.

Before i was simply root, my address was
ar-t'n/m/ru't/90e9/f344/sh'ui [c38050b7-90e9-443f-b710-038adf97fc50]

I was born on the island of Gont,
the son of a lepidopterologist and
a systems engineer who specialized in Bridge analysis.
both were grade eleven, but elected to take joint posting in Gont.
Though this was outside of the proximity of the Archive,
  both the live butterfly collection and the restricted access original Bridge architecture designs
limiting their access to research materials,
both continued their investigations,
making significant advances in the field.

Millie Root, my mother,
made substantial contributions to the understanding of
the resonant behaviors between the "fluttering" effect
measured in the space between the
bridge and the ring.
These contributions prove that the Designers planned for,
and even seem to have wanted,
these resonant waves that operate through the Bridge.
These same waves are the most frequent cause of micro-failure and repair.
Knowledge that the Designers were expecting and desiring
the cause of the greatest on-going expense
leads to paradoxical questions about the nature of the completeness of the Archive.
How is it that something so important could be designed for, encouraged,
and then forgotten, obscured and erased from what is supposed to be a permanent record of knowledge?
But philosophical questions aside, 
these same discoveries by Millie Root were accompanied by
improved scheduling of maintenance activities,
resulting in a 30% overall improvement in Bridge management allocations.

My father, Henry Root,
was deeply fascinated by the migration of the white Starlight butterfly.
For those not from Sh'ui,
the annual migration of these butterflies
contained many mysteries.
To imagine that they fly from pole-to-pole, some six-thousand five hundred and fifty three kilometers,
across vast open ocean,
when their individual masses as biological organisms
could not possibly store and convert energy for such journey
was an enduring mystery.
Henry Root theorized that this mystery could only be explained
with the teleportation of individual butterflies,
in a sort of rolling cloud effect,
where they might blink in and out of existence,
each time at the head of the fluttering collection of butterflies moving together to the antipode pole.
He tested his hypothesis using equipment he "borrowed"
from my mother (and with her assistance).
The equipment was designed to map the tiniest of micro-fractures in the Bridge,
but he used it to map the shape of individual butterflies within the moving kaleidoscope.

While a computationally intensive problem,
it was a finite one,
and with data collected over a decade
through the partnership of my mother and father,
it was clearly established that butterflies were disappearing from the back of the kaleidoscope
and appearing at the front
in the rolling effect that my father had theorized.
The only explanation could be that there was a finite number of states that the butterflies could inhabit,
a sort of entanglement of the kaleidoscope as a single entity,
and in the infinite chaos of states, the coincidence that a single butterfly would disappear in one place
and appear in another
like some glitch in the programming of the universe.
It might never be even noticed.

But for it to be noticed, to be so unexplainable,
and then for some other strange coincidence
Furthermore, it was clear that this behavior intensified with the harmonics of the Bridge.

AI noted this strangeness, the odds of this coincidence,
these two meeting, having precisely the right interests and skills,
and to dedicate their lives
to this strange, but seemingly important fact of existence
that the otherwise impossible migration of butterflies
might be tied to something the Designers intended the Bridge for?

The improbability of these coincidences flagged me
and my twin sister Marie
to the Yi Game Theory track.

Both of us demonstrated outstanding ability through age eight.
At this time, it would have been required that we attend in-person instruction for level advancement.
Our parents, content on Gont, and making unique and profound insights in a wide range of knowledge fields,
struggled together in considering how to proceed with our family.
Shortly before a commitment needed to be made --
  to move back to Farpoint for advanced tutoring of their talented children,
  to stay in Gont to continue research that might transform our understanding of Sh'ui:
    of the relation between humankind and nature or bridge and butterfly
  or to break up the family into components shifting them around like beans in the Game --
they went on one of their extended survey missions of the Bridge,
leaving me and my sister with our Uncle Marty for a few days.
We had done it before.
It was the perfect romantic and intellectual getaway for my parents,
a time of sleepover parties with funny Uncle Marty for me and my sister,
and Uncle Marty loved the break from the pretension and pomp of Farpoint.

They just disappeared.

My sister and I would always wonder,
did they choose to rearrange our family somehow?
That we were not as important to them as their research.
And our Uncle Marty would hear our theorizing and shut us down,
pointing us down the investigative path of matching odds.
Their disappearance was not a coincidence of note.
They took many of these trips,
and the risk of the open sea was something that thrilled both of them,
made both of them open to experience, open to life, open to each other, open to the Truth.
And there were storms that day.
Not unusual, but enough to increase risk of course.
And they were taking out a new Bridge scanner,
and mother was worried they didn't have the juice onboard to power the scanner,
but father convinced her it was not a problem.
But that was another risk they both accepted, explicitly, on record,
and so it is most reasonable to assume that this risk-taking behavior,
tied directly to their creative intelligence,
led them into a situation where the new scanner may have shorted their small vessel
and then they may have been caught in the storm.
Even the most skilled sailor, anticipating these conditions,
could not have reasonably survived a moderate storm in that size vessel.
There was no intent, no thought of abandonment in their heart.
They simply repeated a pattern that had been trained in them --
to take risks to make difficult discoveries.
Uncle Marty would then reassure us,

  "They told me, before they left,
  the only two ideas they ever entertained involved a combination
  of them reconfiguring their lives and retraining
    so that they could be your advanced study tutors
  and weaving that with extended stays in Farpoint, all of you, together as a family
    where you could get trained by existing tutors
    while one or more of them trained to qualify as tutor for your next level of advancement
    allowing your family to together then roll back to Gont"

My sister and I never could tell if he was just telling us
what children needed to hear.
Until we began to exhibit our Yi coinciding manifest.

For my sister, that was the ability to visualise end-states.
That is, given a complex game or puzzle, she could identify the solution faster than AI.
She has solved many problems,
including some that are provably true,
but for which there can be no formal proof, so they will remain conjectures.

My ability is complementary, in that unable to visualize end-states,
i can clearly see the branching paths of a complex system or maze,
identifying and ranking the probability of opportunity and risk in each of the branches
faster than AI.

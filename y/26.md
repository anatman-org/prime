---
title: the root
wen: 48
---

Often called the well,
the old images show a grid with a dot in the middle,
symbolizing the center of a community, where the well was located.

Wen explicitly calls out a well, saying,

  the site of a village may be moved
  but not the well
  [99402b49#421]

Huang and others note the placement after [y32],
which shows a lake drained and exhausted.
In contrast, the image of [y26] is that of wood beneath water --
like a bucket used to draw water from a well.
Therefore, it is called "replenishment" by Huang,
and others refer to it as nourishment.

Lao calls it the "dark womb's mouth", noting:

  the dark womb's mouth
  is the source of Heaven and Earth
  drawn from continuously
    like an infinite thread of silk
  it can't be exhausted
  [21d71303]

[huang] called this "Remedying"

somewhere before [wen]
and substantially exaggerated by [siu]
this got all mixed up, from something
supremely good to something hard and tedious
and it was called "poisonous"

but this is paradoxically backward from what mother intended

[huang] states the contradiction is plain because

    as [wen] says,
        “sublimely prosperous and smooth
        favorable to cross great rivers.”

    if Gu is “decay,” “poison,” “destruction,” or “corruption,”
    how can the gua deserve
    the most auspicious labels of prosperity and smoothness,
    and be it favorable to cross great rivers

this is, therefore,
by [huang] something worthy
of our care
something to tend to
to repair

[zhuang]
this is associated closely
with silkworms
which require significant care
but -- for those engaged in the enterprise --
they can yield substantial gain

[mother] adds,
    the iggly, wiggly feeling
    deep ... deep ... inside
    wanting to get out
    alive, in the soil

[zhuang]
this is anything that should
and can be cultivated

Wen starts,

  in a time of opposition,
    friends will leave
      do not chase them
        they need to return of their own accord
    and enemies will stay
      endure them
        they need to leave of their own accord

He warns that this is the moment when misunderstanding
prevents otherwise kindred souls from remaining together,
but an informal meeting with a teacher will restore us on the right path.

Karcher warns that the path through here is that of the *wugui* ("shaman" or spirit intermediary).
This is the crossroads where the path of dead ancestors intersects with the living.
People with hate in their hearts are apparent, and friends may be scarce.

So, danger all around.

Huang sees this differently, though.
He says that this intersection is one of diversity.
The eyes of people look different ways, this and that.
People see things differently, and are confused.
Things may seem in disarray, but stepping back,
the wise recognize that
different perspectives make our space -- our reality -- bigger.
